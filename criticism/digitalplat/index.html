Rebuttal: DigitalPlat's "Transparency Report"

Mehmet – May 23, 2026

DigitalPlat published a "Transparency & Clarification Report". Below is my direct response, based on tests I personally performed and publicly documented.

⚠️ Core Issue – Their Own Words Contradict Their Claims

The report admits: "ns2 experiences regional instability due to limited VPS resources. We currently lack the budget for additional infrastructure."

Yet their website still claims "global infrastructure" and "24/7 production systems". You cannot claim "global" while admitting you can't afford a working second nameserver.

1. The "Regional Instability" Myth

DNS is global. A nameserver either answers queries or it doesn't. There is no "regional" timeout. My tests on May 23, 2026 at 10:18 AM (UTC+3) show:

$ dig @ns2.digitalplat.org digitalplat.org ;; communications error to 150.230.46.101#53: timed out ;; no servers could be reached

This is not "regional instability". This is a dead nameserver. If your budget cannot keep ns2 alive, do not call yourself "global infrastructure".

2. ns3 and ns4 Still Return REFUSED

The report claims ns3 and ns4 are "Stable" and "Active". My tests from the same morning:

$ dig @ns3.digitalplat.org digitalplat.org ;; status: REFUSED $ dig @ns4.digitalplat.org digitalplat.org ;; status: REFUSED

A nameserver that returns REFUSED for its own domain (digitalplat.org) is not "stable". It is refusing to do its job. Run the same commands yourself. The result will not change.

3. Authoritative Nameservers Are Cloudflare – Not Yours

The report ignores a verifiable fact. Public DNS records show:

$ dig digitalplat.org NS digitalplat.org. 21600 IN NS kenia.ns.cloudflare.com. digitalplat.org. 21600 IN NS tony.ns.cloudflare.com.

Your authoritative nameservers belong to Cloudflare. The names ns1, ns2, ns3, ns4 are just labels. You do not operate your own DNS infrastructure. You are reselling Cloudflare and calling it your own.

4. The "Foundation" Claim Remains Misleading

The report says: "Fiscal sponsorship under a verified 501(c)(3), with EIN and IRS records publicly verifiable."

That EIN (81-2908499) belongs to The Hack Foundation, not to DigitalPlat. DigitalPlat has no legal entity of its own. Calling yourself a "Foundation" while operating under fiscal sponsorship is misleading. Update your website to clearly state: "DigitalPlat is a project fiscally sponsored by The Hack Foundation."

5. The .KG Registry Warning Is Still Missing from Your "Transparency" Report

Your own GitHub repository documented an official warning from the .KG registry on October 16, 2024, due to "malicious activities (including Bank Hacking)".

This warning is completely absent from your "Transparency Report". A report that omits its own registry warning is not transparent. Explain this warning or remove the word "transparency" from your report title.

6. Invitation to Verify (I Already Did)

The report invites readers to run verification commands. I ran them. The results:

  • ❌ ns2 is dead (timeout)

DigitalPlat's "Transparency Report" does not change the technical reality. It admits budget constraints, ignores REFUSED responses, and avoids the registry warning. A long report with uptime badges does not replace working nameservers or honest organizational labeling.

My criticism was and remains: Your marketing claims do not match your technical reality. That is not an opinion. It is a verifiable fact.
