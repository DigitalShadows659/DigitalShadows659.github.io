Social Media: The Slavery Disguised as Freedom

Mehmet Çelik - May 23, 2026

Freedom, connection, expression — that's what social media promised. So what actually happened? More loneliness, more exhaustion, more addiction. The "like" button has become our digital shackle.

⚠️ Short Summary: Social media claims to "connect" us, but in reality it has severed our real bonds. Algorithms have turned our attention into a commodity, reducing each of us to mere "content producers." This is where digital slavery begins.
"If you're not liked, if you're ignored, do you really exist?"

1. Loneliness Has Never Been More Profitable

We've become lonelier than ever — all while surrounded by crowds. Social media platforms have destroyed real connections within a sea of "followers." Face-to-face conversations have been replaced by silent crowds staring at screens. The deeper the loneliness, the more time we spend on these platforms. And that time translates directly into their profit.

2. The Attention Economy and the Algorithms That Steal Our Brains

Your attention is now a commodity. Your most valuable resource — time — is a tool for corporations to sell you things. Algorithms exploit your anger, fear, and curiosity just to keep you scrolling. "The more you scroll, the more you earn them." This cycle turns you into an unconscious slave — a "content consumer" — without you even noticing.

"Scroll, like, share, comment — but never think. Thinking is the algorithm’s greatest enemy."

2. The Myth of Freedom: Expression or Exhibition?

Social media promised us the freedom to express ourselves. But are we truly free? Every post, every preference, every single like is harvested as data, profiled, and sold back to us. In a world where others curate what we see, our autonomy steadily erodes. Our digital identity has started to overshadow our real one.

4. Existence in the Age of Performance

Like count, follower count, engagement rate… These aren't just numbers — they've become the new measure of our existence. If we don't get enough likes, if we aren't seen enough, we start to feel as if we don't exist. This constant need for approval drains our self‑esteem and inner peace. We're living in a circus where we're constantly watched and constantly judged.

5. How Real Bonds Are Breaking

We sit next to each other in silence, staring at our phones. We're at dinner, but we're not present. We talk, but we don't listen. Social media sells us the illusion of being "connected to everyone" while weakening the bonds we have with the people right next to us. Inside digital crowds, we're condemned to a real, tangible silence.

"Wake up. The world is outside the glass under your fingertips. There's still a life waiting there."

Conclusion: Let's Recognize Our Digital Shackles

We don't have to abandon social media completely. But we must learn to use it consciously, knowing exactly what it is. By limiting our screen time, turning off notifications, and engaging only with content that truly adds value, we can stop being cogs in this machine.

Remember: You are not a text. You are not your like count. You are not the identity that algorithms force on you. You are beyond the screen. Being free sometimes means stopping the scroll and simply opening a window.
